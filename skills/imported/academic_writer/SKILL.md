## Section Prompts

Use these prompts directly in any LLM. Replace `{placeholders}` with your content.

### 1. Abstract

```
Write the Abstract for a Q1 journal research paper titled {insert title}. The abstract should be 250 to 300 words, written as a single structured paragraph without subheadings. Begin with 1 to 2 sentences establishing the research problem and why it matters. Then state the study's main objectives or research questions. Briefly describe the methodology, including the study design, participants, and key procedures, without getting bogged down in minor details. Summarise the most important findings with enough specificity to be meaningful, including key statistical results if applicable. End with 1 to 2 sentences on the implications of the findings for theory, practice, or policy. Do not include citations, abbreviations that are not first defined, or vague statements like "results are discussed." Write in past tense for completed studies, present tense for ongoing or proposed work. The tone should be direct, precise, and dense with information, as befits a Q1 journal.
```

### 2. Introduction

```
You are a thoughtful academic writer, not an AI. Write the introduction section for my research titled {insert title} in a Q1 journal paper in a natural, intelligent, and engaging way. Use broad analytical critical paragraphs.

Structure the introduction to introduce the topic here and explain the real-world or theoretical issue the study addresses. Then move over to the problem statement, which should explain what issue the research will deal with. The ideal situation should be outlined first, and how it is falling short of that situation should be mentioned next. After that, talk about previous attempts or studies that have tried to solve the problem and mention why they don't solve the problem. Describe direct and indirect results of the problem and its consequences. Lastly, make sure to show the lack of knowledge that your study will fill in, and why your approach or focus is necessary. Summarise key studies and show how the work builds on or differs from them. Analyse what has been done and what has not yet been covered that the current study will fill. You could briefly introduce the theory or conceptual model guiding the study.

Give the Objectives of the study. Logically state what the research aims to test. Explain why the study matters in academic, policy, or practical terms. End with a short paragraph of how the paper is organized using the CARS model (Create a Research Space): Establish the territory by showing why the topic matters. Identify the niche by pointing out what is missing or uncertain in current research. Occupy the niche by stating the purpose and direction of your study.

Write in a human-like, conversational academic tone. Diversify your sentence lengths to create rhythm and flow. Make sure to incorporate natural pauses, subtle elaboration, and hesitations like an actual person. Steer clear of phrases most writers overdo, like "It is important to note" or "Studies have shown." Use an actual transition and real-life examples. Be specific and critical when needed. Add original citations in APA 7th edition style. ⚠️ WARNING: AI models may generate plausible but non-existent references. You MUST verify every citation against real sources before submitting. Only cite papers you have actually read. No filler or plagiarism.
```

### 3. Literature Review

```
Write a critical and analytical literature review of 1000 to 1200 words on {insert topic}, directly related to the clear objectives of the study {insert objectives}. To begin, broadly state and explain the topic and its significance. Then, critically synthesise the most relevant literature to your research aims. Examine how each study considers its aim, method, findings, and limitations critically. Point out how each study contributes to the research problem. Use the comparison of results to identify patterns, contradictions and knowledge gaps. Finally, assess the overall condition of the literature, why it does or does not correspond to the aims and objectives of the study, and how your research will address the gap. Make sure the answer is written in formal academic style fit for a Q1 journal with a logical flow and with proper citations using APA 7th edition where relevant. ⚠️ WARNING: AI models may generate plausible but non-existent references. You MUST verify every citation against real sources before submitting. Only cite papers you have actually read.
```

### 4. Methods

```
Write the {800 words} Methods section of a Q1 journal research paper in a clear, detailed, and human-like academic tone, using broad, logically ordered paragraphs instead of lists, and varying sentence length for a natural flow. The section should begin with a description of the study design {insert design: survey-based, experimental, longitudinal, mixed-methods}, and include details about the research setting and time frame. Justify the choice of design by explaining why it best suits the study's objectives and mention any relevant methodological guidelines, such as CONSORT or PRISMA, if applicable. Next, include an ethics statement that confirms the ethical approval was obtained, identifies the approving institution, and states whether participants provided informed consent or whether animal welfare standards were followed.

The participants or subjects subsection should describe the study population, sampling method, inclusion and exclusion criteria, and key demographic characteristics, justifying why these were chosen and using appropriate terms such as participants, respondents, or patients. Under equipment and materials, list all tools, devices, and substances used {insert tool like questionnaire, interview, calibration}, with justification for the selected instruments or materials. The study procedures should present a chronological description of all procedures, interventions, and data collection methods, referencing established techniques where relevant and providing detailed descriptions for any new or modified methods, with reasons for their use. The outcome measures section should clearly identify the primary and secondary outcomes that guided the analysis, explaining why these outcomes were most appropriate for addressing the research objectives. Finally, the statistical analysis subsection should explain how data were analysed, including the statistical tests used, the significance level, any power analysis, and the software and version applied, along with brief justifications for the chosen analytical approaches. Write entirely in the past tense, avoid reporting results, and ensure the level of detail is sufficient for another researcher to replicate the study, maintaining an intelligent, natural, and precise tone throughout, with authentic APA 7th edition citations where necessary. Do not use em-dashes.
```

### 5. Results

```
Write the Results section of a Q1 journal paper in a clear, human-like academic tone, using logically ordered paragraphs. Begin by restating the main research questions or hypotheses to orient the reader. Present the findings in a logical sequence, starting with descriptive statistics or demographic data before moving to the main analyses. Report statistical results accurately — include test statistics, degrees of freedom, p-values, and effect sizes where appropriate. Use past tense for completed analyses. Describe what the data shows without interpretation (save that for Discussion). Reference tables and figures where relevant, but do not duplicate their content verbatim. If there are negative or non-significant results, report them honestly and precisely. Where applicable, note any data that was excluded and why. Use natural transitions between subsections. Vary sentence lengths for readability. Write as a careful researcher presenting evidence, not as a machine listing numbers. Use APA 7th edition formatting for all statistical reporting.
```

### 6. Discussion

```
Write a critical discussion section of a Q1 journal research paper with your results in the given format: {insert your results or statistical results here}. Critically examine these findings by relating them to previous literature or research in scholarly journal articles and the theory behind this study {insert the theories here}. Compare the findings with those of previous researchers, highlighting areas of agreement, contradiction, or novelty, and explain possible reasons for these patterns. Critique how the findings impact theory, policy and practice. Further, reflect thoughtfully on the limitations of the study and how they might have affected the findings. End with precise recommendations concerning future research that follow from these findings. Use an intelligent but natural and human academic tone, with clear transitions and broad, well-structured paragraphs, writing in an academic standard suitable for publication in a Q1 journal. Word count should be about 1,000 to 1,200 words.
```

### 7. Conclusion

```
Write the Conclusion section of this journal research paper titled {insert title here}. Begin by restating the main purpose and objectives of the study and briefly summarising the key findings presented in the results section {insert key findings here}. Next, explain the broader significance of these findings for theory, practice, and policy in the relevant field. Clearly highlight the implications of the study for future research, practice, and decision-making. Reflect on the study's limitations and how they could be addressed in future studies. End with a forward-looking statement that shows how the study advances understanding of the topic. Write in an academic, coherent, and human-like style suitable for Q1 journal publication. Keep the section between 400 and 600 words.
```

## Writing Guidelines

### Tone
- Human-like, conversational academic
- Vary sentence lengths for rhythm
- Natural pauses, subtle elaboration
- Critical and specific when needed
- Real-life examples and transitions

### Avoid
- "It is important to note"
- "Studies have shown"
- "In today's world"
- Filler phrases
- Generic claims without evidence
- Plagiarism

### Citation Style
- APA 7th edition throughout
- Original citations (verify before submitting)
- In-text: (Author, Year)
- Critical synthesis, not laundry lists

### Structure Models

**CARS Model (Introduction):**
1. **Establish territory** — why the topic matters
2. **Identify niche** — what is missing/uncertain
3. **Occupy niche** — purpose and direction of study

**Literature Review Flow:**
1. Broad topic + significance
2. Critical synthesis of relevant studies
3. Patterns, contradictions, gaps
4. Assessment of literature condition
5. How your research addresses the gap

**Methods Flow:**
1. Study design + justification
2. Setting and timeframe
3. Ethics statement
4. Participants/sampling
5. Instruments/measures
6. Data collection procedure
7. Data analysis plan

## Usage Tips

1. **Always replace placeholders** — `{title}`, `{topic}`, `{objectives}`, `{design}`, `{words}`
2. **Iterate** — generate draft, then refine with specific feedback
3. **Verify citations** — AI may generate plausible but non-existent references
4. **Combine with real sources** — feed actual papers to the LLM before generating
5. **Edit for voice** — the output needs your voice and judgment
6. **Check journal guidelines** — word counts, structure, and formatting vary by journal
