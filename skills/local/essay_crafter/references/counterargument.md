# Counterargument Protocol

Every argumentative or problem-solution essay MUST address at least one significant opposing position.

## Identification Protocol

Locate opposing positions from:

- Literature dissent — scholars who reject or qualify the thesis
- Methodological critiques — objections to the evidence or methods used
- Alternative frameworks — competing explanations or paradigms

Prioritize the strongest objection available. Weak opposition weakens the essay.

## Steel-Manning Template

Before rebutting, present the opposing position at its strongest:

1. State the opposing claim in its most compelling form
2. Cite the evidence or reasoning that supports it
3. Acknowledge any valid points it raises

Do not attack a straw-man version. The rebuttal is only credible if the steel-man is credible.

## Rebuttal Structure

Three required components:

1. **Concession** — acknowledge the valid points raised by the counterargument
2. **Distinction** — explain why the thesis still holds despite the concession (new evidence, different scope, stronger reasoning)
3. **Reaffirmation** — restate the original position with the counterargument integrated (the thesis is now nuanced, not abandoned)

## Integration Patterns

- **Standalone section** — place between the last development section and the conclusion. Preferred for essays over 2000 words.
- **Inline** — embed within a development section where the counterargument directly challenges that section's claim. Acceptable for essays under 2000 words.

## Decision Rule

If no counterargument is addressed, the structure gate MUST fail. The essay is incomplete until at least one opposing position is engaged with the steel-man + rebuttal structure above.
