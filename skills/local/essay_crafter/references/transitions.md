# Transition Strategies

Concrete techniques for building bridges between essay sections. Use alongside `structure_gate.md` transition checks.

## Strategies

### Signposting

Explicitly flag what is coming or what was done.

- "Having established X, the analysis now turns to Y."
- "The next section examines Y in light of the findings above."
- "This section has argued X; the question that follows is whether Y."

Use sparingly. Overuse produces a mechanical feel.

### Backward reference

Summarize or invoke the prior section's conclusion before advancing.

- "The correlation identified in the previous section raises a further question: ..."
- "If the data pattern described above holds, then ..."
- "That tension between X and Y sets up the central puzzle of this section."

### Forward reference

Plant an expectation early and fulfill it later.

- "Section 4 returns to this methodological concern."
- "As the counterargument will show, this assumption is not uncontested."
- "The implications of this finding become clearer in the synthesis."

Always fulfill planted expectations. Unfulfilled forward references erode trust.

### Conceptual bridging

Connect sections through shared concepts, metaphors, or analytical frames.

- "The same boundary problem that limits X also constrains Y."
- "Where the regulatory approach treats the issue as one of compliance, the ethical lens reframes it as one of responsibility."
- "Both frameworks share an assumption that deserves scrutiny: ..."

## By essay section

### Introduction to first body section

Restate the roadmap commitment and open the first analytical move.

- "The first dimension to examine is X, which grounds the subsequent analysis."
- "To evaluate this claim, the evidence must first address X."

### Between body sections

Link the conclusion of one section to the question of the next.

- "X, then, is well supported. Whether Y follows from it is less clear."
- "These findings complicate the earlier assumption that ..."
- "The regulatory picture sketched above sets the stage for a deeper normative question."

### Last body section to conclusion

Signal synthesis, not just summary.

- "Taken together, these dimensions point to a qualified affirmative."
- "The evidence across all three dimensions converges on a single implication."

## Validation

If a section boundary lacks at least one of these strategies, the transition is implicit. Add a bridge sentence before moving on.
