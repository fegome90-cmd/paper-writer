# Essay Outline Template

Use this scaffold before drafting.

## Essay Type

Select one: `argumentative` | `comparative` | `chronological` | `thematic` | `problem-solution`

Default: `argumentative`

## Question

- What exact question is the essay answering?

## Thesis

- What position will the essay defend?

## Roadmap Sentence

- Write one sentence that announces the thesis and the order of the major sections.

## Introduction Micro-Structure

Every introduction must include these four components in order:

### 1. Hook (choose one)

- **Provocative claim** — state a surprising or contested fact.
- **Anecdote** — open with a brief relevant scene or episode.
- **Contrast** — juxtapose two opposing positions or expectations.
- **Statistic** — lead with a striking data point.
- **Question** — pose the research question directly.

### 2. Context-building scope

What field, debate, or phenomenon does the essay enter? What background does the reader need?

### 3. Scope delimitation

What the essay covers and what it deliberately excludes. One sentence is sufficient.

### 4. Thesis placement

The thesis is the last sentence of the introduction. It must be contestable, not a fact or a plan.

## Section Map

### Argumentative (default)

1. **Introduction** — Hook, context, scope, thesis
2. **Empirical Evidence** — What does the evidence show? What are its limits?
3. **Epistemic / Data Constraints** — Fragility, bias, incompleteness
4. **Governance / Regulation / Ethics** — Institutional controls
5. **Normative Synthesis** — Criteria and recommendations
6. **Counterargument** — Steel-man, concession, distinction, reaffirmation
7. **Conclusion** — Restatement, synthesis, implications (per conclusion micro-structure below)

### Comparative

1. **Introduction** — Hook, context, scope, thesis
2. **Criteria Definition** — Establish dimensions of comparison
3. **Subject A Analysis** — Evaluate against each criterion
4. **Subject B Analysis** — Evaluate against same criteria
5. **Synthesis** — Side-by-side comparison
6. **Counterargument** — Challenge the comparative conclusion
7. **Conclusion** — Verdict grounded in criteria

### Chronological

1. **Introduction** — Hook, context, scope, thesis
2. **Phase 1** — Earliest period: conditions, events, significance
3. **Phase 2** — Transition: what changed and why
4. **Phase N** — Each phase marked by temporal boundary
5. **Causal Analysis** — Link phases, identify drivers of change
6. **Counterargument** — Challenge the causal narrative
7. **Conclusion** — What the trajectory reveals

### Thematic

1. **Introduction** — Hook, context, scope, thesis
2. **Theme 1** — First analytical lens across the topic
3. **Theme 2** — Second lens (may cut across chronology)
4. **Theme N** — Additional themes as needed
5. **Convergence** — Where themes intersect or conflict
6. **Counterargument** — Challenge the thematic framing
7. **Conclusion** — Synthesis of thematic findings

### Problem-Solution

1. **Introduction** — Hook, context, scope, thesis
2. **Problem Definition** — Scope, severity, stakeholders affected
3. **Root Causes** — Why does the problem persist?
4. **Solution Proposal** — Mechanism, feasibility, evidence of effectiveness
5. **Implementation Challenges** — Barriers, trade-offs, mitigation
6. **Counterargument** — Why the solution might fail or alternatives exist
7. **Conclusion** — Viability assessment and conditions for success

## Conclusion Micro-Structure

Every conclusion must include these components:

### 1. Thesis restatement

Restate the thesis in new language. Do not copy-paste the introduction's thesis sentence.

### 2. Synthesis of main arguments

Show how body sections converge to support the thesis. Not a summary list — a synthesis.

### 3. Acknowledgment of limitations
Name at least one limitation of the evidence or analysis. Honesty strengthens credibility.

### 4. Broader implications

Why does this argument matter beyond the essay's immediate scope?

### 5. Forward-looking statement (optional)

A closing line pointing to future research or open questions. Omit if it feels forced.
